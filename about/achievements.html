Sales Evidence & Weekly Reports

Documenting our consistent sales progress from Week 6 up to our Expo Day in Week 10.

Week 6

This week marked the exciting start of our operations! As a new business, our primary focus was on perfecting our recipe and making sure our premium Matcha and Chocolate Moist Cakes reached our very first buyers safely. Honestly, in the rush and excitement of fulfilling our first batch of pre-orders, we completely forgot to take pictures with our customers! It was a humbling reminder that while operations are important, capturing the marketing moments is just as crucial.

Week 7

Growth comes with growing pains. This week, we expanded our sales directly to the KKTPAR (Kolej Kediaman Tun Pengiran Ahmad Raffae) area. With the sudden influx of interest, we faced a major operational hurdle: managing messy and forgotten orders. It was a stressful week, but a highly educational one. We quickly realized the importance of strictly utilizing our Google Form pre-order system and tightening our team communication to ensure no customer was left behind

Week 8

Our operational adjustments from the previous week paid off immensely. Thanks to word of mouth and our social media marketing, our "Double Matcha" signature cakes started gaining serious traction . We experienced a surge of crowded orders across campus. Fulfilling this high volume tested our time management and teamwork, but successfully delivering so many high-quality cakes proved that our business model and problem-solving strategies were working.

Week 9

To maximize efficiency and manage the high demand, we adopted a decentralized "divide and conquer" strategy. Instead of centralizing all deliveries, each of our 7 team members took charge of selling and delivering the cakes individually at their respective residential colleges and faculties. This approach not only made the delivery process much faster but also allowed us to connect with our customers on a more personal level across the UMS campus.

Week 10 (Expo)

The grand finale of our entrepreneurial journey! Expo Day was an incredible experience where we set up our physical booth to showcase Sedap Cravings Pastry Chef to the wider university public. We brought our best cakes, engaged directly with the crowd, and applied all the marketing and sales skills we had refined over the past weeks. It was a proud, full-circle moment for the team, celebrating our hard work, our sales achievements, and every lesson learned throughout this project
